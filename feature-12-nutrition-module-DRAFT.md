## 1. The child this is built for

A girl in the same Mymensingh village as the mother in the Maa paper, fourteen
months old now. She was born at 2.4 kilograms, which nobody recorded. She is
weighed occasionally, at an immunization visit, at a growth monitoring session
when a program passes through, and each weight is written in a register that
never meets the previous one. Each number, alone, looks almost fine. The curve
they would draw together, falling away from the reference line for nine
consecutive months, exists in no place and is seen by no one. At her five-year
checkup, if she has one, she will be counted: one more stunted child in the
28%. Everything that could have changed the count happened between fourteen
months and thirty, in the window nobody was watching.

## 2. The evidence

**Bangladesh is off course on every maternal, infant, and young child
nutrition target.** The Global Nutrition Report's country profile, built on
the UNICEF, WHO, and World Bank Joint Malnutrition Estimates, records stunting
at 28.0% of children under five (against an Asia average of 21.8%), wasting at
9.8% (against 8.9% regionally), low birth weight at 27.8% of infants, and
exclusive breastfeeding at 62.6%. Anaemia affects 36.7% of women of
reproductive age, and on that indicator the monitoring records no progress at
all. These are not famine numbers. They are the numbers of a country whose
food security improved while its nutrition surveillance never existed.

**The national framework is at a hinge point.** The second National Plan of
Action for Nutrition (NPAN2) ran 2016 to 2025 and has reached the end of its
term. The successor instrument is being written now, which means the
instruments that get named in it will define a decade of programming and
funding. UNICEF's own mid-term evaluation of the national nutrition program
documents the persistent operational complaint: multisectoral coordination on
paper, fragmented delivery and fragmented data in practice.

**Nutrition data arrives years late, by survey.** The sector measures itself
through periodic household surveys. A stunting figure published this year
describes children measured one to three years ago. Program managers steer by
a rearview mirror, and funders evaluate against baselines that were already
stale at contract signature. The chronic gap every nutrition actor names is
not food, knowledge, or even money. It is timely, individual-level,
longitudinal measurement, which is precisely the thing a clinical record
network produces as a side effect of operating.

**The workforce constraint is the familiar one.** The community health worker
lattice that the Maa paper builds on (government FWAs and HAs, NGO CHWs) is
the same lattice every nutrition intervention depends on. A MUAC tape costs
pennies. A solar scale costs less than a blood pressure cuff. The constraint
was never equipment; it is that the measurement, once taken, lands in a paper
register and dies there.

## 3. The module: what it does

**The same argument as the blood pressure, at a slower tempo.** Maa's central
claim is that the stalled cause of maternal death is the detectable one:
pressure, measurable by a trained neighbor, weeks before it kills. Stunting is
the same claim stretched over months. It announces itself in a growth curve
that nobody plots. The module's entire job is to plot it.

**In pregnancy (Maa).** Gestational weight tracked at every contact on the
existing surveillance calendar. Anaemia screening alongside the blood
pressure, with iron and folate adherence riding the same WhatsApp reminder
rails as the visit schedule, addressed to the mother and the family circle
that decides what she eats and whether she rests. Low birth weight, the 27.8%
number, lands in the newborn's first credential at delivery, which is exactly
the flag that should set the follow-up cadence for the first year.

**After birth (the newborn path, extended).** Weight-for-age at every
immunization contact, captured by whoever holds the child: the EPI vaccinator,
the CHW, the Chamber doctor at a sick visit, the Karigor health assistant
seeing a worker's baby. Each weight is a signed observation in the child's
wallet. KhaM-Med plots the trajectory and watches the trend, not the single
value, exactly as it watches blood pressure. Faltering growth, the curve
bending away from the reference line, escalates to the CHW for counseling and
to the facility when counseling is not enough. The child who is becoming
stunted is visible eighteen months before the statistic records it, inside the
window where it can still be changed.

**In the factory (Karigor).** Anaemia screening for a majority-female
industrial workforce, as a routine part of the medical room encounter. The
HERhealth evaluations that anchor the Karigor paper carried nutrition
programming for international buyers before; the channel is proven, and the
buyer funding precedent with it. The result lands in the worker's wallet, not
the employer's file, under the same absolute line as everything else.

**In the household (Pocket).** Plain Bangla answers to the questions families
actually ask at the moment of doubt: what to feed the child at six months,
whether the weakness is normal, when it is not. Exclusive breastfeeding sits
at 62.6%; counseling at the moment of decision, in the family's own language,
is what the IYCF literature says moves it. Pocket does not lecture; it
answers, and it escalates the red flags (the visibly wasted child, the infant
who stops feeding) with the same firmness as chest pain.

## 4. Why this needs the identity layer

A growth curve is, by definition, longitudinal: it exists only if every
measurement, taken by different hands at different places over years, lands on
one identity. That is the network's founding capability. The newborn
credential from the Maa paper is the anchor; every weight after it is an
observation signed by whoever took it, with provenance labeled. And the
sensitivity is real even here: a child's malnutrition status disclosed
carelessly stigmatizes a family; a worker's anaemia result belongs to her and
not her employer. The same patient-held keys and shielded categories govern.

## 5. Economics and the funding pathway

The module is free at the point of care, always, for the same reason Maa is.
Its delivery costs are marginal by design: it rides contacts that already
happen (ANC visits, EPI sessions, factory encounters) and adds equipment
costing less than the blood pressure cuffs the Maa pilot already budgets.

The funder map is distinct from every other Glyph stream and unusually dense:
UNICEF and WFP, GAIN, Nutrition International, the World Bank's multisectoral
nutrition lending, and the Government of Bangladesh's own NPAN successor
programming. What this module offers them is the thing the sector cannot buy
today: consented, real-time, individual-level, longitudinal nutrition
surveillance, produced as a byproduct of care rather than as a survey. The
fundable pilot is the Maa pilot's upazila with the nutrition spine switched
on, measured against the district's most recent survey baseline. Realistic
dedicated funding: USD 1 to 3M as a rider on the Maa pilot, or a standalone
demonstration grant from a single nutrition funder.

## 6. Honest constraints

- **Measurement does not feed anyone.** Like Maa, the module detects and
  routes; it does not treat. Where the escalation endpoint is therapeutic
  feeding that the upazila facility does not stock, the module delivers a
  well-documented child to an empty shelf. Facility readiness belongs in the
  routing table here exactly as it does for magnesium sulfate.
- **Scales and tapes must physically exist.** The pilot budget buys them; the
  scale model rides existing CHW and EPI equipment or grant-funded
  distribution. No instrument, no spine.
- **Growth standards are not neutral.** Plotting against WHO reference curves
  is standard practice and still requires clinical framing in counseling;
  KhaM-Med's guidance follows national IMCI and IYCF protocol, and the
  escalation thresholds are set with clinical review, not invented.
- **The stigma risk is the privacy risk.** Aggregate community nutrition
  reporting uses the same minimum-cell-size suppression as Karigor's
  compliance reports. A village's stunting rate is public-health data; a
  named child's is not.
- **This module tempts mission creep.** Nutrition programming spans
  agriculture, WASH, and social protection. The module's boundary is stated:
  it measures, counsels, and routes within the clinical network. It does not
  become a food program, and the document says so.

## 7. Build status (June 2026)

Designed at brief level, not specified. It inherits nearly everything: Maa's
surveillance calendar and escalation engine, the assisted-capture flow, the
newborn credential, the family circles, the WhatsApp rails, and the wallet.
Its specific new pieces are small: growth-standard curves and trend logic in
KhaM-Med's watching layer, MUAC and weight capture screens in the CHW and
Karigor flows, IYCF counseling content in plain Bangla, and the
anaemia-screening loop. Sequenced with Maa, because it is Maa's second spine,
and timed to the NPAN2 successor cycle, because instruments named in national
plans are instruments that get funded.
