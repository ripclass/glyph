## Why mirror RulHub /writing

It's not a blog — it's a **worldview argued in public**. Three load-bearing moves:

1. **One thesis at the top that every piece serves.** RulHub: *"a probabilistic model cannot
   serve a domain that runs on certainty… the same engine serves every regulated domain."*
2. **Two tiers.** ~4 white papers (foundational, 9–14 min) + ~20 numbered essays (one sharp
   idea each, 5–8 min). Numbering signals a *body of thought*, not scattered posts.
3. **Taglines that state a truth, not a topic.** *"The safe answer is 'I could not check.'"*
   Concrete, declarative, no hedging. Sell the idea; the product is the idea made real.

Deepest move: RulHub essays 01–06 = *"domain X falls out of the same primitives."*
KhaM's exact analogue: **one spine (verifiable health identity + portable patient-owned record)
→ every health setting** (Chamber, Pocket, Pharmacy, Lens, Hospital, Maa, Continuity, Karigor,
Bridge). Each module *falls out* of the spine. That IS the "one engine, every domain" series.

## Proposed thesis (Claude's recommendation — to reconcile with Ripon's)

> Essays on what health software owes the people it serves. One argument runs through all of
> them: a patient's record should belong to the patient, be portable, and be verifiable by
> anyone — and the model should augment the clinician's judgment, never replace it, and never
> leave the country.

## White papers (4 — the foundations)

1. **The record belongs to the patient** — why the verifiable credential is canonical and the
   database is only a projection.
2. **Where the model belongs** — the division of labor between AI, the doctor, and the
   signature. Glyph never diagnoses, never prescribes. (Direct analogue of RulHub's piece.)
3. **Sovereign by necessity** — why a Bangladeshi health AI must run on Bangladeshi terms:
   data residency, KhaM-Med, PDPO 2025.
4. **Meeting the clinic that exists** — 1 doctor per 1,400, paper Rx pads, the family
   attendant, the plastic bag of old prescriptions.

## Essays (numbered, one idea each — starter set)

- 01 **One spine, every setting** — how Lens, Hospital, Maa all fall out of identity + record.
- 02 **The plastic bag** — how records actually travel in Bangladesh.
- 03 **Why we never write SOAP** — CC / O-E / Ix / Rx / Advice, meeting doctors where they are.
- 04 **The attendant speaks for the patient** — source-tagging every claim.
- 05 **Consent before the model sees anything** — the egress gate as a feature.
- 06 **A credential a pharmacist can verify in seconds** — revocation that propagates.
- 07 **Why WhatsApp, not an app** — meeting patients on the channel they already live on.
- 08 **Knowing the patient before they walk in** — the briefing card.
- 09 **Glyph never prescribes** — the safety invariant, stated plainly.
- 10 **One human, one identity** — the identity layer; why DID per person, not per record.
- Then one essay per module (each "this setting falls out of the spine"):
  Lens (labs), Hospital (discharge), Pocket (the patient's wallet), Maa (antenatal),
  Continuity (clearance/portability), Karigor (occupational health), Bridge (specialist opinion).
