# Nutrition, GRS, and HSS
## Research brief: what the UNICEF advice means for Glyph

**Glyph by KhaM Health · Research Brief · June 2026 · Dhaka**
**Prepared in response to advisory input from a former UNICEF official**

---

## The one-paragraph answer

The advice is not "add three products." It is "speak the language donors evaluate in." Health System Strengthening (HSS) is the framework donors use to decide what is infrastructure and what is a gadget; Glyph already is HSS and should say so in the donor's own grid. A Grievance Redress System (GRS) is a standing requirement of donor-funded projects and a documented national failure Glyph's architecture fixes almost for free. Nutrition is the one genuinely new clinical domain, and it slots into Maa, Karigor, and Pocket the same way blood pressure did: a cheap measurement, a trend nobody watches, a surveillance gap with a body count.

---

## 1. Nutrition: the evidence and the fit

**Bangladesh is off course on every maternal and child nutrition target it has set.** The Global Nutrition Report's Bangladesh profile, built on the UNICEF/WHO/World Bank Joint Malnutrition Estimates, reads: stunting at 28.0% of children under five (above the Asia average of 21.8%), wasting at 9.8% (above the regional 8.9%), low birth weight at 27.8% of infants, and anaemia at 36.7% of women of reproductive age, a number on which the report records no progress at all. The country's multisectoral framework, the second National Plan of Action for Nutrition (NPAN2, 2016-2025), has just reached the end of its term, which means the successor planning cycle is happening now. That is a window: new instruments get written into new plans.

**Why this fits the existing architecture rather than demanding a new one.** The Maa paper's central argument is that the stalled cause of maternal death is the detectable one: blood pressure, measurable by a trained neighbor with a Tk 2,500 cuff. Nutrition is the same argument at a slower tempo. Stunting announces itself in a growth curve that nobody plots, because the weights are taken at scattered contacts and recorded nowhere. Anaemia in a pregnant woman is a test strip and a trend. A MUAC tape costs pennies and a community health worker already knows how to use it. What is missing is identical to what was missing for preeclampsia: longitudinal capture, a watched trend, and escalation that arrives before the emergency does.

**Concretely, the touchpoints already exist in the product set:**

- **Maa** gains a nutrition spine alongside the BP spine: gestational weight tracking, anaemia screening and iron-folate adherence (the 36.7% number), and counseling timed to the same WHO-aligned contact schedule. Low birth weight (27.8%) lands in the newborn's first credential, which is exactly the flag the follow-up schedule needs.
- **The newborn path** extends naturally from immunization to growth monitoring: weight-for-age at every EPI contact, plotted automatically, with faltering growth escalated the way a climbing BP is. The child who is becoming stunted is visible eighteen months before the statistic records it.
- **Karigor** adds workforce nutrition: anaemia screening for a majority-female workforce, and the precedent is already proven, because the same peer-educator channel evaluated in Bangladeshi factories (the HERhealth finding the Karigor paper rests on) has carried nutrition programming for international buyers.
- **Pocket** answers the question families actually ask in plain Bangla: what to feed the child, whether the weakness is normal, when it is not. Exclusive breastfeeding sits at 62.6%; counseling at the moment of doubt is what moves it.

**The funder map is distinct and dense:** UNICEF and WFP, GAIN, Nutrition International, and the World Bank's multisectoral nutrition lending all fund exactly this, and they fund measurement infrastructure especially well, because the sector's chronic complaint is that nutrition data arrives years late through surveys. A network that produces consented, real-time, longitudinal growth and anaemia data as a side effect of care would be, for these funders, the most interesting thing about it.

**Recommendation:** a nutrition module inside Maa and Karigor, not a twelfth product. Write it as a product document (the Maa paper's structure carries it), add a section to the Maa page, and name it only if and when a dedicated funding conversation demands a name.

## 2. GRS: a documented national failure the architecture already fixes

**What it is.** A Grievance Redress System is the formal channel through which the people a service claims to serve can complain, and through which the complaint demonstrably reaches resolution. In the donor world this is not optional decoration: the World Bank's Environmental and Social Framework requires functioning grievance mechanisms in the projects it funds, and the Bank operates its own Grievance Redress Service above that. Accountability to affected populations is standing UNICEF and UN-wide policy. If Maa or Continuity ever takes institutional money, a GRS will be a compliance requirement on day one.

**Bangladesh already has one, and its performance is the now-familiar pattern.** The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare runs a national health GRS. A 2025 PLOS Digital Health study analyzed 11,604 anonymous messages submitted to it between January and August 2023: 60.3% were complaints, mostly about workforce, infrastructure, and service delivery. The system's median first response was fast, under four hours. But the responses themselves tell the story: 67.0% of messages were simply forwarded to another department, 30.1% were closed, and 2.55% were resolved. The more serious the complaint, the less likely it was to be resolved. This is the Lens QR-code finding again, in a different uniform: the state has the instinct and built the channel, and the channel forwards instead of fixing, because nothing binds a complaint to an accountable resolution.

**Why Glyph fixes this almost for free.** A grievance is a claim by an identified (or deliberately anonymous) person about an identified service event, requiring a tracked, attributable resolution. That is a credential flow, and every primitive it needs already exists in the network: the patient's identity, the encounter record the complaint refers to, the anonymous mode (enrollment path H) for the complaints people are afraid to sign, selective disclosure so a funder can see resolution statistics without seeing complainants, and the append-only audit trail that makes "resolved" mean something. A complaint filed in Pocket about a Chamber visit, a Karigor worker's grievance about the factory medical room, a Maa mother's report that the facility she was routed to turned her away: each becomes a signed record that cannot be quietly forwarded into oblivion, with aggregate resolution rates visible to exactly the parties who should see them.

**Recommendation:** build GRS as a network-wide capability, not a product. Specify it as a short product document (the credential schema is a week of design, not a moonshot), present it on the Identity & Matching page as the accountability layer, and lead with it in any donor proposal, because it converts a compliance checkbox every funder requires into a differentiator nobody else in the country can offer: a grievance system where resolution is cryptographically attested rather than self-reported, against a national baseline of 2.55%.

## 3. HSS: not a product, the donor's evaluation grid

**What it is.** Health System Strengthening is the WHO framework that defines a health system as six building blocks: service delivery, health workforce, health information systems, access to essential medicines, financing, and leadership and governance. It is the lens through which Gavi (whose dedicated HSS funding window has run for nearly two decades), the Global Fund (under the label RSSH, resilient and sustainable systems for health), and the World Bank decide whether something is system infrastructure worth public money or a vertical product that dies when the grant ends. Gavi and the World Bank announced a deepened HSS collaboration as recently as December 2025. When a former UNICEF official says "add HSS," he is saying: I cannot route you to money until you describe yourself in this grid.

**Glyph already maps onto all six blocks.** This is the reframe, and it costs nothing but a page:

| WHO building block | What Glyph contributes |
|---|---|
| Health information systems | The identity layer and patient-held record: the missing national HIS, built bottom-up, PDPO-compliant by construction |
| Health workforce | Verifiable professional identity against BMDC's own stated registry gaps; the matching engine; clinical upskilling of CHWs, karigor health assistants, and dispensers |
| Access to essential medicines | Pharmacy's verification loop against the 50-92% non-prescription antibiotic baselines; dispensing records that make stewardship enforceable |
| Service delivery | Chamber, Lens, Continuity, Maa, Hospital: preparation, diagnostics, routing, and continuity at the point of care |
| Financing | Direct attack on the waste inside 73% out-of-pocket spending: duplicate diagnostics, wrong first stops, medication errors; the ADB health-DPI modelling already prices this in the hundreds of millions annually |
| Leadership and governance | Verifiable compliance instruments for BMDC, DGDA, DGHS, and DIFE; the GRS accountability layer; published pilot data offered to regulators |

**Recommendation:** one new page, written for this audience: "For governments and funders" (or simply /partners), carrying the six-block mapping, the GRS commitment, the nutrition extension, and the succession clause from the identity paper, which is precisely the governance assurance institutional funders look for and almost never find in a private operator. The same content becomes the one-pager for the next UNICEF-adjacent conversation.

## What this brief recommends, in order

1. Reframe first: write the partners page with the six-building-block mapping. Zero new engineering, immediate donor fluency.
2. Specify GRS as a credential flow and present it on the identity page. Small build, large differentiator, and a hard requirement of the funding Maa and Continuity already target.
3. Extend Maa and Karigor with the nutrition module and write the product document, timed to the NPAN2 successor cycle. This is the genuinely new clinical work, and it is the one with a dedicated funder map.
